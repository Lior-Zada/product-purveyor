# AC discovery (split, inverter)

AC is **the dominant power user in IL homes during summer.** A high-COP inverter can pay back its premium in 2-3 summers. Energy class + COP are the most important specs &mdash; not BTU.

## Discovery questions

1. **Single split or multi-split?**
   - **Single split** &mdash; one indoor unit + one outdoor unit. Best efficiency per room.
   - **Multi-split** (mini-VRF) &mdash; one outdoor unit serves 2-5 indoor heads. Saves balcony/wall space, slightly less efficient overall, more expensive per unit.
   - For 1-2 indoor units in different rooms: prefer two singles unless balcony space is constrained.
2. **BTU per room** &mdash; sized to room area + sun exposure + ceiling height:
   - Bedroom 12-18 sqm, north-facing: 9,000-12,000 BTU
   - Bedroom 12-18 sqm, south-facing: 12,000-15,000 BTU
   - Living room 25-40 sqm: 18,000-24,000 BTU
   - Open-plan 40-60 sqm: 24,000-30,000 BTU
   - **Don't oversize** &mdash; oversized units short-cycle, dehumidify poorly, waste energy. Slightly under-sizing on inverter is fine.
3. **Energy class (Israeli label)**:
   - **A or A+** = best
   - B = acceptable
   - C-G = avoid in IL where AC runs 4-5 months/year
4. **COP / EER ratings**:
   - Cooling COP &gt; 4.0 = efficient
   - Cooling COP &gt; 5.0 = excellent
   - Heating COP &gt; 4.0 = will work as primary winter heat in coastal IL
5. **Use as winter heater?** &mdash; If yes, verify heating COP and ensure outdoor unit handles low temperatures (most IL units are fine; just check spec for &gt;-7&deg;C operation).
6. **Wi-Fi** &mdash; **actually useful for AC** &mdash; turn on before getting home, schedule for sleep, Siri/Google Home control. Worth the small premium.
7. **Aesthetic constraints**: indoor unit color/profile? Some living rooms want the slim "designer" units (Tornado Inspire, LG Artcool, Tadiran Supreme); they cost ~+₪500.
8. **Indoor noise level** &mdash; aim for &lt;26 dB on low fan in bedrooms.
9. **Self-cleaning evaporator** &mdash; reduces mold/odor; nice to have.
10. **Brand preference / installation included?** &mdash; most retailers offer installation; budget separately.
11. **Warranty preference**: official import 5-7y on compressor (best); yevuan moy 1-2y (avoid for AC).
12. **Budget per unit + installation**: hard cap, soft target.

## Defaults to assume

- Voltage: 220V
- Circuit: **16A dedicated per indoor unit** (some 18-24k BTU need 20A) &mdash; **verify with electrician before buying** if existing wiring isn't AC-ready
- Indoor unit: wall-mounted (vs cassette / floor / ducted &mdash; specialty)
- Refrigerant: R32 (modern standard, lower GWP than older R410A)
- Installation cost (haranha): **₪800-1500 per unit standard run**, more for long copper pipe runs (&gt;5m), wall penetration through stone, or top-floor outdoor placement

## Features that matter (and why)

- **Inverter compressor** &mdash; *not optional in modern AC.* Variable-speed, ~30-50% more efficient than fixed-speed, much quieter. Every reputable brand uses inverter on mid+ models.
- **Cooling COP &gt; 4.5** &mdash; over a 12-year IL lifespan, COP 4.0 vs 5.5 = ₪3,000+ in electricity savings. Worth +₪1,500-2,000 upfront on a 12k-BTU unit.
- **Wi-Fi** &mdash; usability multiplier. Schedule, remote-on, integration. Worth the premium.
- **Self-cleaning evaporator** &mdash; reduces musty smell after summer; less frequent professional cleaning.
- **Quiet mode** &mdash; sub-22 dB on low fan; bedroom-friendly.
- **Sleep program** &mdash; gradual temperature creep through the night; saves energy and feels comfortable.

## Common Israeli pitfalls

- **Tornado** &mdash; IL official, very common, decent service network. Some Inspire-line series had premature compressor failures around year 5-6. Cross-check year-specific reviews.
- **Tadiran** &mdash; longest-established IL brand; good service network, mid-tier engineering. Safe choice.
- **Electra** &mdash; like Tadiran, IL native; service network reliable.
- **LG / Samsung** &mdash; better engineering, better COP, but IL service network depends on the importer (LG official is solid; Samsung official is mixed).
- **Daikin** &mdash; premium engineering, best efficiency in class, but IL service is limited &mdash; mostly for new construction with system installation by Daikin partners. Risky for retail purchase unless you have an importer relationship.
- **Yevuan moy AC** &mdash; **avoid.** Compressor failures at year 5-6 are common; out-of-warranty repair is ₪3,000-5,000 vs replacement at ₪4,000-7,000. Official 5-7y compressor warranty is the actual product.
- **Installer matters as much as the brand.** A good installation makes a great unit perform; a bad installation makes a great unit fail. Use the manufacturer's recommended installer or a vendor's in-house team. Avoid the cheapest haranha quote.
- **Indoor unit placement** &mdash; cold air drops; mount high, away from where you sit. For long rooms, position so cold throw covers the longest dimension.
- **Outdoor unit placement** &mdash; needs airflow clearance; cramped balcony or shaded recess kills efficiency.
- **Multi-split limits** &mdash; outdoor unit can usually drive 4-5 indoors but **simultaneous max** is rated; over-spec'ing means underperformance when all rooms are on.
- **R22 retrofits** &mdash; old buildings sometimes have R22 lines; new units are R32 and incompatible. Plan to re-run lines.

## Value model overrides

- Default lifespan: **12y official import**, **9y yevuan moy** (compressor failure ends practical life)
- Annual energy cost (IL rates @ ₪0.65/kWh):
  - 12k BTU bedroom, COP 4.0, ~3 hr/day in summer: **~700 kWh/yr = ₪455/yr**
  - 12k BTU bedroom, COP 5.5, same use: **~510 kWh/yr = ₪330/yr** (~₪125/yr savings)
  - 24k BTU living room, COP 4.0, ~5 hr/day in summer: **~1,400 kWh/yr = ₪910**
  - 24k BTU living room, COP 5.5, same use: **~1,020 kWh/yr = ₪660**
- Installation amortization: ₪1,000 / 12y = ~₪85/yr add to TCO
- Whole-home AC TCO over 12 years often runs ₪10,000-25,000 in electricity alone &mdash; bigger than the units' purchase prices. **Pay for COP.**

## Upsell triggers

- **COP &lt;4.5 chosen for &lt;₪1,500 delta vs &gt;5.0**: suggest the upgrade. Frame: *"You spend ₪Y less per year for 12 years &mdash; that's ₪Z saved. Pays back in 4 years."*
- **Yevuan moy chosen for any AC**: strongly suggest official import. Frame: *"Compressor fails year 5, repair is ₪4,000 vs ₪0 on official. Almost never the right call to save 15%."*
- **Single-split chosen but room has divider/can be partitioned**: suggest dual mini-split. Frame: *"Cool only the room you're in. Annual savings often ₪500+ vs blasting one big unit."*
- **No WiFi chosen**: gentle nudge. *"₪200-400 for remote-on; you'll use it every summer day."*

## Output checklist

When writing the item file, ensure:

- `category: ac`
- `constraints` includes `btu_per_room` (or `btu_min`), `cop_min`, `multi_split` (bool), and per-room context if multiple rooms
- `selected` has `brand`, `model`, `price_ils`, `vendor`, `product_url`, `image_url`, `warranty`, `tashlumim_available`, `link_verified_date`
- `selected.features` &mdash; 4&ndash;6 entries. For AC always cover: BTU, cooling COP, energy class, inverter compressor, wifi (if present), refrigerant type (R32). Each `detail` quantifies the benefit (₪/yr saved, dB on low fan, etc.).
- `selected.reviews` with sources (each with `url`) and 2&ndash;3 specific quotes (incl. compressor reliability or noise if mentioned)
- `alternatives_considered` populated with 2&ndash;3 options, each with full structure plus specific `why_skipped` (cite COP, brand service network, or noise)
- `upsell_considered` populated &mdash; for AC, the COP upsell is almost always live
- `energy.kwh_per_year` populated based on BTU, COP, and use hours
- `energy.est_annual_cost_ils`: kWh &times; 0.65
- `expected_lifespan_years`: 12 official / 9 yevuan moy
- `replacement_due` calculated
- `power_amps: 16` per indoor unit (24k BTU may need 20A)
- **Installation cost (haranha) recorded separately** &mdash; typically ₪800-1500/unit; flag if vendor quote includes it
